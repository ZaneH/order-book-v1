* Glossary
- side: the trade type (buy or sell)
- tick: the smallest unit of upwards/downwards movement of an asset
  - Example: In USD, $1.00 has a tick size of $0.01
- level: a collection of orders at a given tick value, crucial for displaying market depth (L2)
- FIFO: first-in-first-out
- best bid/ask:
  - bid: the highest price a buyer is willing to pay for an asset
  - ask: the lowest price a seller is willing to accept for an asset
  - Think "highest bidder, lowest asking price"
- spread: the difference between the best bid and best ask
- cross: when the bid is greater than the asking price ("cross"), the engine can instantly match these trades
